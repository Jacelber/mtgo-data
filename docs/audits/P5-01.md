## Contract decisions

The whitelist contract requires an explicit event ID, Melee URL, date range, Constructed format, series, event structure, review and enabled states, phase assignments, statistical scope, source evidence, and special-handling notes.

The normalized-event contract keeps these axes independent:

- `stage`: Day 1, Day 2, playoff, or other;
- `round_phase`: Draft, Constructed, playoff, or unknown;
- `game_format`: the actual format, such as Limited or Modern.

This prevents the Draft Top 8 from being mistaken for a Modern playoff. Unknown phases and results require explicit quality issues. A blocked quality state cannot be marked publishable.

Player source IDs and display names remain available for event-scoped joins among standings, decklists, and matches. Stored test fixtures use synthetic identities. Publication and raw-data tracking policies remain later controlled decisions.
